Project objectives:

Improving the prediction of opinion distribution in a target society by means of topological analysis, temporal and spatial distribution of opinion sources, and real-time simulation on empirically gathered data.

  • O1: Topological analysis of empirical social network data to understand how interconnection patterns of individuals and communities influence the spread of opinion.

  • O2: Development of an innovative social interaction model, inspired by previous original work, considering the temporal aspect of opinion sources.

  • O3: Definition of a strategy for real-time opinion seed selection by means of node and edge centrality distribution.

  • O4: Synergy of results from O1-O3 with direct applicative socio-economic impact by developing a crowdsourcing web-platform for gathering anonymized empirical data from users (e.g., opinion, time, location).

Estimated results:

The targeted deliverables of this project are 1 highly visible journal publication (D1), and 2 publications (D2, D3) at international conferences. Another deliverable is a web platform/phone app (D4) for online voting on diverse socio-cultural topics that will be used both for gathering data, and for research during the project period, as well as serve as a free platform offering individuals unbiased information about public opinion on important topics.

D2 and D3 will focus on delivering intermediary research results from the expected findings on how opinion spreaders work, based on motifs analysis, respectively experimentation of a new agent interaction model based on temporal injection of opinion. D1 will encompass validated research on how opinion spreading can be predicted, and what the key elements are that influence the complex diffusion process.
